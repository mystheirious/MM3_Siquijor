About Our Island

Siquijor Island, located in the Central Visayas region of the Philippines, is a hidden gem known for its pristine beaches, waterfalls, caves, and diving spots. Beyond its natural wonders, the island is rich in folklore and traditions, with locals practicing ancient healing rituals that add to its enchanting charm. The Spanish once called it the “Island of Fire” due to the glowing swarms of fireflies that light up the night. Home to warm and hospitable Siquijodnons, Siquijor offers both adventure and cultural discovery. Whether you're exploring its landscapes or immersing yourself in its mystic heritage, this island paradise promises an unforgettable experience.

Experiences you can't miss in Siquijor

Discover Siquijor’s Mystical Traditions

World of ancient healing

Siquijor is known for its healing practices, where traditional healers use herbal remedies, spiritual rituals, and “white magic” to cure ailments and attract good fortune. You can visit local healers, explore the mystical side of the island, and even witness the annual Healing Festival during Holy Week.

Immerse Yourself in Nature’s Beauty

Explore and relax

From pristine beaches like Salagdoong and Paliton to breathtaking waterfalls like Cambugahay, Siquijor is a paradise for nature lovers. You can swim, snorkel, cliff dive, or simply relax by the crystal-clear waters. The island also has enchanting caves, lush forests, and marine sanctuaries waiting to be explored.

Experience Local Culture and History

Island’s rich heritage

Siquijor is home to centuries-old churches, Spanish-era landmarks, and warm, hospitable locals. You can visit St. Isidore de Labrador Church, explore the Lazi Convent, and learn about the island’s rich history and folklore. The laid-back vibe and strong community spirit make every visit feel special.
